# Synthesis Gates (Cogworks-Encode)

Use these gates when producing synthesized knowledge from multiple sources.

## Fidelity Gates
- Preserve source intent without distortion.
- Keep key distinctions explicit (do not flatten meaningful differences).
- Flag contradictions explicitly and resolve with rationale.

## Output Contract Gates
- Required sections:
  - TL;DR
  - Decision Rules
  - Anti-Patterns
  - Quick Reference
  - Sources
- Add optional sections only when they add unique decision value.

## Source Scope Gates
- Label source scope:
  - Primary platform (normative)
  - Supporting foundations (normative when applicable)
  - Cross-platform contrast (non-normative)
- Cross-platform contrast may inform tradeoffs but must not override primary guidance.

## Citation Gates
- Ensure factual claims and normative rules are source-backed.
- Keep citation references stable and resolvable.

## Compression Gates
- One canonical location per fact.
- Remove repeated prose and section quota inflation.
- Keep protective nuance (edge-case distinctions, constraints).

## Edge-Case Gates
- Sparse sources: state thin coverage and confidence limits.
- Conflicting sources: surface conflict and chosen interpretation.
- Broad domain overlap: recommend split into multiple focused syntheses.
